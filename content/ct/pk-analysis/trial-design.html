Tofacitinib pcJIA

BACK

BASELINE DISEASE CHARACTERISTICS

International, open-label Phase 1, multiple dose study evaluating pharmacokinetics, safety, and tolerability (N = 26)a

Key inclusion criteria
  • Age 2-<18 years
  • Active pcJIA (extended oligoarthritis, RF+/-, jPsA or ERA
  • ≥ active joints at time of enrollment
Key exclusion criteria
  • Systemic JIA
  • Persistent oligoarthritis
  • Undifferentiated JIA
  • History of autoimmune disease or any condition affecting drug absorption
Endpoints

PK endpoints

  • AUCtau(ng·h/mL)
  • Cmax(ng/mL)
  • Tmax(h)
  • T1/2(h)
  • CL/F (L/h)

Safety endpoints:

  • Adverse Events
  • Vital Signs
  • Lab Data
  1. Children aged 2 to 6 weighing <30 kg and those 6 to 18 weighing <40 kg received oral solution, while children aged 6 to 18 weighing ≥40 kg received tablets, although oral solution (1 mg/mL suspension) was offered to those who preferred a solution to a tablet.

ACR, American college of Rheumatology; APR, acute phase reactant; AUCtau, area under the plasma concertation time curve; BID, twice daily; CHAQ DI, child health assessment questionnaire disability index; CL/F, apparent systemic clearance; Cmax, maximum observed plasma concentration; ERA, enthesitis related arthritis; JIA, juvenile idiopathic arthritis; jPsA =juvenile psoriatic arthritis; pcJIA, polyarticular course juvenile idiopathic arthritis; Tmax, time of maximum concentration observed.

Ruperto N, et al. Pediatr Rheumatol Online J. 2017;15:86.
